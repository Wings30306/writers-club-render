Ratings Guide

This guide is meant to help you decide what rating is appropriate for your story. Though we hope that it's clear enough to be self-explanatory, we're happy to answer any questions you might have.

If you wish us to look over a segment of your story and help you determine the appropriate rating, please send the segment in the body of the email (NOT as an attachment) to that email address for our review.

Anything not permitted in these Guidelines at a particular category is unacceptable also at all preceding lower categories. Similarly, anything permitted at one level is acceptable at all higher levels.

The ratings used on the Writers' Club are as follows:

All Ages

Appropriate for all ages. This signifies that the story rated contains nothing most parents will consider offensive for even their youngest children to read or have read to them. Nudity, sex scenes, and scenes of drug use are absent; violence is minimal; dialogue may go beyond polite conversation but do not go beyond common everyday expressions.

PG - Parental Guidance Suggested

Some material may not be suitable for children, but is suitable for all registered users on the Writers club. This signifies that the story rated may contain some material parents might not like to expose to their young children - material that will clearly need to be examined or inquired about before children are allowed to read the story. Explicit sex scenes and scenes of drug use are absent; nudity, if present, is described only briefly, horror and violence do not exceed moderate levels.

PG-13 - Parents Strongly Cautioned.

Some material may be inappropriate for children under 13, but all material is appropriate for all registered users on FA. This signifies that the story rated may be inappropriate for pre-teens. Parents should be especially careful about letting their younger children read it. Rough or persistent violence is absent; violence towards fantasy characters and/or people rendered in cartoon-style is permitted; sexually-oriented nudity is generally absent, although sexual activity may be implied and non-sexually oriented nudity is permissible if not described in a graphic manner. Sexual references may reflect the familiarity of most adolescents today with sex education through school. Some scenes of drug use may be described and some use of the harsher sexually derived words may be included; the strongest terms will be acceptable only where justified by the context.

15 - Restricted to those 15 years old and over

No theme is prohibited, and there may be frequent use of strong language (eg 'fuck'), especially if justified by the context, although repeated use of graphic terms for body parts, in connection with action verbs, will merit an R-rating. There are no constraints on descriptions or depictions of nudify in a non-sexual context. Sexual activity may be portrayed but without strong detail. Violence may be strong but may not dwell on the infliction of pain or injury. Dangerous techniques (eg combat, hanging, suicide and self-harming) should not dwell on imitable detail. Strong threat and menace are permitted, as are written descriptions of gory material, but intensely gory images will merit an R-rating. Drug taking may be shown but the work as a whole must not promote or encourage drug misuse.

R - Restricted (Adult-only / NSFW)

Children under 17 should generally not read this fic without parental permission, as it contains adult material. An R may be assigned due to, among other things, a story's use of language, theme, violence, sex, or its portrayal of drug use. While there are no restrictions on nonsexually oriented nudity, as explained in the previous section, and while sexual activity and nudity may be portrayed, the use of the most graphic terms for such depictions is not permissible in R-rated stories. There may be occasional strong verbal references to sexual behaviour. Violence may be strong but should not dwell on detailed and realistic descriptions of the infliction of pain. Scenes of sexual violence must be handled carefully. Drug taking may be shown but instructive, imitable detail is unacceptable. Dangerous techniques (examples include: combat, hanging, suicides) should contain no instructive and imitable detail. We recommend use of the "fade to black" technique, instead of use of action verbs, in describing otherwise-explicit sexual interactions. We, or the author may place an additional warning for strong material at the top of a submission or in the header's Author Notes.
